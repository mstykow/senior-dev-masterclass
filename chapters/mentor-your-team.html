Summary

💡 If you can't explain it you don't understand it.

💡 Mentoring is the fast(er) track to mastery.

💡 Don't feel guilty about the guilt pile. Consumption is LLA.

💡 Get to the bottom of the learning pyramid.

Reduce your bus factor

The more influential your role is, the more you have to think about succession planning.

  • Succession planning is not about knowledge sharing
  • Hard to let go of influence/power
  • Trust that the natural process of evolution will present you with new challenges
  • Steve Jobs 2005 Stanford Commencement Address: connecting the dots
  • Inverse bus factor: how many need to get hit by a bus for the project to get back on track?

💡 Identify and develop individuals for leadership roles

💡 Leaders grow leaders (not followers)

💡 As a consultant, your job is to enable the client to do your job. If you want to build everything yourself, you have no business being a consultant.

💡 Mentoring pyramid: avoid most experienced dev mentoring the least experienced

  • Must let go of your ego but there is a reward: trust, reciprocity, recognition
Exercise

Rank the following tools that affect employee motivation:

  • Support for making progress in the work
  • Recognition for good work
  • Incentives
  • Interpersonal support
  • Clear goals
  • 700 managers from around the world
  • only 5% ranked "making progress" first
  • most ranked "recognition" first, followed by "incentives"

Mentor's Toolbox

🧰

1/5: Support

Guide • Connect • Sponsor • Encourage • Recognize

🔴 Thanks

🟡 Thank you!

🟢 Thank you, Hans!

❤️ Thank you, Hans, for shipping the new feature on time and with great quality! Keep rocking!

🚀 Recognize Hans publicly

🙏🏼 Create a system where people recognize each other's work

  • connect: use your network, do 1-on-1s
  • sponsor: buy a book, don't just tell them to give a talk but help them find a topic
  • encourage: help people remember a time where they overcame an obstacle
  • recognize: a common reason people leave jobs is due to lack of appreciation
  • Remember situational leadership? Don't be the same leader towards all.
  • We are often so obsessed with PR review that we forget to admire each other's work. When was the last time you praised one of your team mates?
  • Instill and support enthusiasm
  • Behind every request hides an unsatisfied need. Even if you cannot fulfill the request, can you address the need?
2/5: Challenge
  • Delegate technical and non-technical work
  • Delegate tasks that maximize learning (trade-off with speed)
  • Delegate the most interesting work
  • Remember: your job is to make yourself unneeded by teaching people how to solve their own problems rather than solving them for them
  • Your ego will insist that you do. Your potential will insist that you delegate.

Instead of "How can I do this?", ask "Who is the best person that can do this for me/us?"

🙋🏼‍♀️: How can I make this code less complex?

👨🏼‍🏫: Where do you see complexity? What options are you aware of?

🙋🏽: Where are the passwords located?

👩‍🏫: Where have you looked?

🙋🏻‍♂️: John writes crappy code. Life sucks.

👩🏻‍🏫: What are you going to do about it?

3/5: Share

🧠 Knowledge

⚡️ Energy

📈 Optimism

🤨 Doubts

  • If you don't have optimism inside of yourself it's probably time for a change.
  • A leader saying "I don't know" gives permission to others to also say "I don't know"
4/5: Set Rules
  • Less micro-management, more systems
  • YBIYRI (DevOps)
  • CI/CD
  • QA should find nothing
  • Definition of Ready/Done
  • WiP limits
  • Team leads should have the power to set rules. Without power, you cannot solve problems. But with power comes responsibility.
5/5: Build Trust

Trust is the product of:

  • Knowledge
  • Consistency
  • Authority
  • Confidence (mental/physical health, ability to manage stress/anxiety)
  • The company you keep
  • Charisma (energy, body language 55%, voice 38%, words 7%)
  • Communication skills
  • Your EQ (art of managing mind, thoughts, emotions, empathy)
  • Accountability (own your mistakes)
